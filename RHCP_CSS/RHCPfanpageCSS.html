Red Hot Chili Peppers

Biography


The Red Hot Chili Peppers are a rock bank formed in 1983 in Los Angeles. Considered one of the most successful alt-rock bands in history, they won six Grammy Awards and were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame in 2012.

The band consists of the following members

  • Lead Singer: Anthony Kiedis
  • Bassist: Flea
  • Drummer: Chad Smith
  • Guitarist: John Frusciante
